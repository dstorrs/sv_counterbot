    Looking at this quest like an RTS instead of as just an RPG, opens up the strategic elements that are present in MtG. However, both the spawn mechanic, and the way resources are acquired reminds me of Civilization, more than say, Age of Empires, or Starcraft. Like all RTS, we have to decide our win condition, and then how we want to get there. Due to the fluid nature of the map we are in, I see little benefit in going for a military victory, or even a diplomatic one. when your neighbours are in constant flux, enemies and allies both might not exist on the next turn. (There is even the chance that we Chaos Shift out). The only sure thing we have is a tech victory, as even losing an entire hive only cripples how fast we are progressing. The problem now is researching down the tree in an optimal fashion, this is further complicated by us having access to not just our own "sliver tech" but potentiality every single tech tree for every race we encounter.
